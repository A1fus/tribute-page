Pierre de Fermat

The world's greatest amatuer mathematician.

A portrait of Pierre de Fermat, a French mathematician and lawyer. Author unknown.


Pierre de Fermat was born sometime at the end of 1607. He was from Gascony, where his father, Dominique Fermat, was a wealthy leather merchant. He attended the University of Orléans from 1623 and received a bachelor in civil law in 1626, before moving to Bordeaux. He is credited with effectively invented modern number theory. Some of his many theorems include :

  • The Two Square Theorem, which shows that any prime number of the form 4n+1 can always be re-written as the sum of two square numbers.
  • Fermat's Little Theorem, which states that for two given numbers a and p where p is prime and not a factor of a, then a p-1 will always leave a remainder of 1.
  • Fermat numbers, while not a theorem, are a subset of the integers of the form 22n+1, where n is a nonnegative integer. Fermat numbers are prime ∀ n ≤ 4, but all Fermat numbers tested after 4 have been nonprime.
